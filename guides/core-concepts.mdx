## Understanding Matched Betting

Matched betting is a mathematical strategy that converts free bets and promotional offers from sportsbooks into guaranteed cash profits. Unlike traditional gambling, matched betting eliminates risk by covering all possible outcomes of an event.

<Info>
**Key Principle:** By placing opposing bets on the same event across different platforms, you ensure a profit regardless of the outcome. This is possible because sportsbooks offer promotional bets that have real cash value.
</Info>

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Back Bet">

</Tab>

<Tab title="Lay Bet">

Betting AGAINST an outcome (betting exchange)

</Tab>

## Types of Arbitrage Betting

There are two main types of arbitrage betting strategies, each with different profit potentials and risk profiles.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Bonus Arbitrage (Matched Betting)">

The most profitable form of arbitrage that leverages sportsbook promotions like free bets, bonus bets, and risk-free offers.

**Benefits:**
- Higher profit margins (typically 70-80% of free bet value)
- Uses promotional offers as leverage
- Completely risk-free when done correctly

</Tab>

<Tab title="Odds Arbitrage">

Exploiting small discrepancies in odds across different sportsbooks to guarantee small profits.

**Characteristics:**
- Lower profit margins (typically 1-5%)
- Requires larger bankrolls
- More time-sensitive opportunities

</Tab>
</Tabs>

## Risk Management Fundamentals

While matched betting is designed to be risk-free, proper risk management ensures you protect your profits and avoid common pitfalls.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
<Card title="Common Risks to Avoid" icon="alert-triangle">
- Human error in calculations or bet placement
- Account limitations or closures
- Voided bets or rule changes
- Insufficient bankroll management
</Card>

<Card title="Best Practices" icon="check-circle">
- Always double-check calculations before placing bets
- Start with smaller amounts until comfortable
- Keep detailed records of all bets and profits
- Diversify across multiple sportsbooks
</Card>
</CardGroup>
